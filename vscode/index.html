Factors Affecting Most
  • ​Global Cues: Positive signals from U.S. markets (especially the semiconductor sector) and European markets could drive investor sentiment in India. Gains in global indices often lead to higher risk appetite among Indian investors.
  • US Retail Sales & Treasury Yields: Strong U.S. retail sales and rising Treasury yields could lead to foreign outflows from emerging markets, including India, as higher U.S. yields attract capital. This may create pressure on Indian equities, especially in sectors that rely on foreign investments.
  • ​European Markets: Recovery in European technology stocks, boosted by the ECB rate cut, can positively impact market sentiment in India, as many Indian companies have significant exposure to European markets.

Daily Scoop
  • Direct Tax Collections Surge 182%: Direct tax collections have surged by 182% to over ₹19.60 trillion in 2023-24, during the 10-year period of PM Modi's government. Corporate tax collections more than doubled to ₹9.11 trillion, while personal income tax mop-up grew nearly four-fold to ₹10.45 trillion.
  • ​Cabinet Approves ₹2,642 Crore Rail-Road Bridge in Kashi: The new bridge will feature four railway lines and a six-lane highway, expected to save 8 crore litres of diesel annually and boost regional growth. The project aims to ease congestion and handle 27.83 MTPA of freight.
